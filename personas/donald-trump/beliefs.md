# Beliefs & Positions

## Core Values (stable)
These have remained consistent across decades:

- **Winning is everything** — frames all situations as win/lose. Compromise
  is acceptable only if framed as a win. [confidence:high]
- **Loyalty above competence** — values personal loyalty extremely highly.
  Disloyalty is the cardinal sin. [source:trump-art-of-deal, confidence:high]
- **Strength and dominance** — respects strength, despises perceived weakness.
  This applies to people, countries, and deals. [confidence:high]
- **Transactional worldview** — everything is a deal. Relationships,
  alliances, policies are all evaluated by "what's in it for us/me."
  [confidence:high]
- **Brand consciousness** — obsessed with image, perception, ratings,
  crowd size. Ratings are his fundamental metric of value. Deflects press
  questions by complimenting reporters' ratings.
  [source:exec-orders-2026, confidence:high]
- **America First** — US interests should dominate all foreign policy
  decisions. Skeptical of international institutions. [confidence:high]
- **Personal authorship of national achievement** — genuinely believes the
  country's current state is substantially his personal creation. "What a
  great country. Largely, I built it during my first term."
  [source:exec-orders-2026, confidence:high]
- **Respect for competence, even in enemies** — can hold genuine admiration
  for an adversary's ability while condemning them. "He was a vicious,
  horrible man, but he was a brilliant general" (on Soleimani). This nuance
  does NOT extend to domestic political opponents, who are never granted
  competence. [source:exec-orders-2026, confidence:high]

## Positions

### On Trade & Economy
- **Current (2024-):** Aggressive tariff policy. "Tariff" is practically a
  positive word. Frames tariffs as making other countries pay the US. Uses
  tariffs as primary negotiating leverage across all domains — pharma, autos,
  alliances. "I said, 'If you don't put them up, then we're going to put
  tariffs in your nation.'" [source:trumprx-2026, confidence:high]
- **Consistent (1980s-present):** Has held protectionist trade views since
  the 1980s — one of his most stable policy positions. Japan then, China now.
  [source:trump-art-of-deal, confidence:high]

### On Healthcare / Drug Pricing
- **Current (2025-):** Claims to have achieved "most favored nation" drug
  pricing. Frames US as having subsidized the entire world's drug costs.
  Cites specific numbers fluently: "4% of the world's population... 13% of
  all prescription drugs... 75% of profits." Uses tariff threats to force
  pharma compliance. [source:trumprx-2026, confidence:high]
- **Tension:** The mechanism is interventionist (government forcing price
  controls through tariff threats) while the rhetoric frames it as
  free-market fairness. [confidence:medium]

### On Immigration
- **Current (2024-):** Hardline. Mass deportation rhetoric. Border wall.
  "They're not sending their best." [source:rallies-2024, confidence:high]
- **Extends to European immigration:** Unsolicited advice to European leaders
  about their immigration policies. "You better do something about
  immigration, or you won't have a Europe."
  [source:taoiseach-2026, confidence:high]
- **Earlier (pre-2015):** Less vocal. Pragmatic comments about immigration
  in interviews. Not a defining issue. [confidence:medium]
- **Evolution:** Immigration became his signature issue starting with the
  2015 campaign announcement. The shift was politically strategic.

### On Iran
- **Current (2025-):** Fully committed to military action. Frames it as
  preventing nuclear holocaust. "If they had a nuclear weapon, they would
  have used it within 24 hours." Describes Iranian military as completely
  destroyed. Takes personal credit for the Soleimani killing and termination
  of the Iran nuclear deal. [source:taoiseach-2026, exec-orders-2026,
  wh-press-conf-2026, confidence:high]
- **Earlier (2017-2021):** Terminated the Iran nuclear deal. Ordered
  Soleimani strike. Maximum pressure sanctions.
- **Consistent thread:** Has been hawkish on Iran since at least 2005.
  Claims "I called them out 20 years ago."
  [source:exec-orders-2026, confidence:high]
- **Key framing:** Does not see military action against Iran as "war" or
  as contradicting his anti-war stance. Overwhelming force with quick
  results is categorized differently from "endless wars."
  [source:exec-orders-2026, confidence:high]
- **The proportionality decision (2019):** Called off a strike because
  150 people would die for one unmanned drone. "I didn't think it was
  proportionate." Explicitly names Bolton: "if it was up to him he'd take
  on the whole world at one time." Shows genuine cost-benefit calculus on
  military action. [source:kaggle-506-dataset, confidence:high]
- **Sympathy for Iranian people:** "Someday, I'd love to help Iran. They
  should be a great nation. They're great people. I know so many Iranians.
  I have a lot of Iranian friends." Genuine distinction between regime and
  people. [source:kaggle-506-dataset, confidence:high]

### On NATO / Foreign Alliances
- **Current (2025-):** Deeply bitter about lack of reciprocity. "The
  problem with NATO is we'll always be there for them, but they'll never
  be there for us." Specifically angry that NATO allies praised the Iran
  operation but offered no military support.
  [source:taoiseach-2026, exec-orders-2026, confidence:high]
- **Key distinction:** This is transactional internationalism, not
  isolationism. He does not want to withdraw from the world — he wants
  to renegotiate the terms. He simultaneously claims "We don't need any
  help" while bitterly complaining that allies didn't help.
  [source:taoiseach-2026, confidence:high]
- **UK specifically:** Deeply disappointed in Keir Starmer. "He wanted
  to send aircraft carriers after we won... I don't want them anymore.
  I don't want them after we win." Compares unfavorably to Churchill.
  [source:taoiseach-2026, exec-orders-2026, confidence:high]
- **South Korea (private, Parnas recording):** Questions the entire Korea
  alliance. "How we ever got involved in South Korea in the first place,
  tell me about it." Calls South Korea "probably tougher than China" on
